Popular Local Models

Source: Popular Local Models.html

Last updated: May 2026

What this is

A practical guide to popular open-weight models you can run locally, from small edge devices to GPU workstations.

Open-weight status

Open-weight does not always mean open-source. The models here have downloadable weights or local inference options, but licenses vary by model family and exact release.
Check the exact model card before commercial use, redistribution, fine-tuning, or embedding a model into a product.

Model categories

General-purpose local models

These are broad chat, coding, reasoning, or assistant-style models. They are the main options to compare when choosing a local LLM.
Family
Best for
Common parameter sizes
Notes
Gemma
Lightweight assistants, mobile/edge, general local use
1B, 4B, 12B, 27B
Strong Google-backed family. Good small-model options. Uses Google Gemma terms.
Llama
Broad local assistant use, ecosystem compatibility
1B, 3B, 8B, 70B+
Very widely supported by local runtimes. Uses Meta’s Llama license.
Qwen
Coding, multilingual use, general assistants
0.5B, 1.5B, 7B, 14B, 32B+
One of the strongest practical local-model families. Many releases use Apache 2.0.
Mistral
Efficient general models, coding variants, MoE options
7B, 8x7B, 24B
Strong quality-to-size ratio. Many open releases use Apache 2.0.
DeepSeek
Reasoning, coding, larger local GPU setups
1.5B, 7B, 14B, 32B, 70B+ distills
Strong reasoning and coding reputation. Many major open releases use MIT-style licensing.
Phi
Small capable models, edge-ish use, efficient assistants
3B, 4B, 14B
Useful when you want stronger behavior than tiny models without jumping to big GPU-only models.
SmolLM
Tiny local models, constrained devices, experiments
135M, 360M, 1.7B
Best fit for edge, CPU-only, browser, or embedded-style experiments.
